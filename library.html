Dr. Perry’s Books on Primary Health Care:

  • Engaging Communities for Improving Mothers’ and Children’s Health — a comprehensive review of the evidence on how community-based primary health care improves the health of mothers, newborns, and children in resource-constrained settings, drawing on decades of research and field experience.
  • Health for the People: National Community Health Worker Programs from Afghanistan to Zimbabwe — a 478-page reference surveying national community health worker programs around the world. Each country case study follows the same structure, covering history, health needs, program design, supervision, financing, impact, and challenges — a resource without equal for anyone working in community-based primary health care.
